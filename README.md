## How I think about the work
- **Enterprise → consumer, and what transfers.** Enterprise taught me to design
  for fragile operational workflows, stakeholder graphs, and audit trails.
  Consumer forces you to re-learn speed and aesthetic. The overlap — designing
  **internal tooling that serious humans rely on every day** — is where I've
  shipped most.
- **Category-level over case-level.** I publish how a certain kind of PM should
  think about a class of problem, not retrospectives of specific projects.
  Methodology is portable; anecdotes age.
- **Backend PM ≠ engineer.** The job is to develop sharp mental models of
  systems I don't build, so I can scope, prioritize, and measure with engineers
  who do. Most PM writing under-indexes on this skill.
- **AI is leverage, not substance.** The tooling compounds only if the taste
  layer underneath is sharp — which question to ask, which trade-off matters.
